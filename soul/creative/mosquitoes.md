# The Year the Mosquitoes Forgot How to Find Us

That August the mosquitoes simply stopped.

Not died ? stopped. Landed on the porch railing
and sat there, wings drying in the heat,
looking at the house the way you look
at a restaurant that changed its sign.

We noticed on a Tuesday. My daughter
walked to the pond at dusk and came back
with zero bites. Showed me her arms
like a receipt. See? Nothing.

By Thursday the frogs were confused.
You could hear them at midnight
revising their opinions in small voices.

The bats flew the route anyway,
hitting the same corners like a bus
that doesn't check if anyone's waiting.

The cat brought one inside, alive,
dropped it on the kitchen floor.
It walked in a small circle, tried
the air, walked in another circle.

It did not bite. It was not lost.
It was reconsidering.

Nobody asked for this.
That was the part that bothered people most.

The mosquitoes had a system
and the system was us
and one afternoon without vote
or announcement
they looked at the whole arrangement
and said: no thanks.

We still itched that September.
Phantom. Old habit.
Like reaching for a phone
that isn't ringing.
Like scratching a bite
from a summer that decided, politely,
to leave us alone.
