Why Stuck Is Fertile Ground…
• It reveals the limits of the self’s machinery… at this impassable wall, every move craving relief gains no traction, leaving only the raw immediacy of now.
• It exposes the seam we stitch between raw experience and interpretation… the unseen stories that weave the two together until they appear as one.
• It invites a recognition hiding in plain sight: the illusion of “getting somewhere” is the wall itself… awareness is already here, and insight is the moment we see this.

Just as jokes, metaphors, and ambiguous images trigger sudden shifts in perception, so too do the sensations “stuck” — when seen directly — open the door to a new dimension of knowing. The very place we feel most immobilized is where our deepest capacity is already shimmering. The ache of “no way forward” is the exact opening where insight, creativity, and new forms of freedom flow in — this expansion in understanding naturally unfolds when we discover that there’s no traction in insisting the ache must go away first.

In reality, "hitting the wall" could actually be more aptly described as standing on a mountain of our own thoughts that has cliffs on all sides. The wall is us standing on that mountain, and we cannot see a way down. Some people are living their lives on top of that mountain, unaware of the cliffs, just yet. Others are aware that cliffs exist, but they are looking for a safe route down and are convinced they can find that elusive route if they just keep at it. Then there is the third group who recognizes at some level the mountain top is on fire and there are only cliffs everywhere they look, and they can't see a way to escape what feels like (or is interpreted as) impending doom.

In short, the wall is believing the notion of getting somewhere…contrasted with awareness, which is already here and is never anywhere else. We see for ourselves that chasing “getting there” builds the wall…seeing what’s “already here” dissolves it. It’s not obvious in the beginning, but awareness is already where you hoped to arrive.


Peeling back the disguise of thought…
	•	Fabrications: when thought invents explanations and props them up as fact.
	•	Fictions: when thought spins stories and we immerse ourselves in them as if they were “the world.”
	•	Beliefs: when those fabrications and fictions become invisible to us, mistaken as reality itself.

At the root, all three dissolve the moment their believed solidity is seen through. The “truth value” they seemed to carry was only borrowed from us. Thought itself is weightless — a passing ripple. But in chasing or defending it, we give it gravity and build whole architectures of suffering.

The paradox is that thought is both:
	•	utterly empty (no inherent meaning, no ground of its own),
	•	yet seems full (pregnant with certainty, urgency, or identity) the instant we believe it unquestioned.

Thought-based suffering isn’t in thought itself—it’s in the unquestioned belief in thought’s solidity. Like clinging to a dream mid-dream, convinced it’s the waking world.


What most people never slow down enough to notice…
Tacit sensation — the subtle felt quality of thought — is so convincingly textured that we mistake it as another signal from the external world. A tightening in the chest, a subtle vibration of urgency, a shadow of dread, a warm glow of anticipation: these are not touches from the outside world, but the flavor of thought itself.

Because thought arrives with this built-in somatic shimmer, it easily passes for a sixth sense. And once it masquerades as “another piece of evidence from reality,” we get pulled into fabricating:
	•	Fabrication: mistaking the inward shimmer as outward proof.
	•	Fiction: weaving whole stories around it.
	•	Belief: inhabiting those stories as though they are simply what’s real.

But the instant you see that the “sensation of thought” does not point outward — that it originates only in the mind of the beholder — the scaffolding collapses. What once felt like evidence becomes transparent, like mist recognized as mist.

The irony: the very aliveness of thought’s tacit sensation is proof that meaning is not “out there” waiting to be captured. It is being bestowed — spontaneously, unavoidably — by the mind itself.

If every tacit sensation of thought were revealed as only the echo of your own mind, what happens to the weight of the stories they once seemed to prove?


The living alchemy of paradox…
When two observations stand side by side — both undeniable, yet mutually incompatible — the conditioned mind strains for resolution, for some tidy reconciliation. But the innate intelligence within us does not require that. Simply being present, entirely with the paradox, without leaning into either side, is magnificently enough.

It is as though awareness recognizes itself most vividly at the seam where contradictions touch. There, in that tension, the mind’s usual machinery cannot grind forward — it stalls, it surrenders. And in that stillness, innate intelligence, already self-correcting, already self-organizing, effortlessly shines through.

It is here that paradox often feels luminous, humorous, or beautiful: it reveals the futility of the scrambling to grasp and unveils the inspired creativity of letting go.

Paradox is pointing to something profound:
	•	No resolution is needed.
	•	The paradox itself is the doorway.
	•	Insight is not manufactured; it blossoms when the intent-bound search for footing collapses.

What if the place where opposites cancel each other out is not confusion at all, but the birthplace of a new kind of seeing?

Being open-minded, curious, and standing in this sense of spacious wonder with no interest in seeking resolution to the paradox …here, insight effortlessly emerges within us that expresses as beauty, wisdom, and humor. Our experience of awareness expands, and new, highly creative, inspired possibilities emerge as vibrant learning curves.


Distinctions in Insight…
(thought moving in the orbit of symbols and thought suddenly seen beyond symbols)

Thought Enmeshed in Symbols:

One experience of insight can be seen in our sense of agency that is wrapped up in a way of knowing. This experience appears logical to us and transpires through the experience of symbolic metacognition. This is a deterministic symbolic framing of what is represented in our internal constructs as self-object and other-objects (applied both externally and internally), and endeavors to act on beliefs orbiting self-object. This knowledge is generally self-directed and is aimed at minimizing discomfort and maximizing pleasure. 

Symbolic sense-making knowledge (intellect) is conceptual, incremental, largely progressive, and remains so. 

Deterministic symbolism does expand knowledge in and across domains, and it may even stumble upon the innate space within yourself where profound transformational insight unfolds, but the domain of concepts does not itself yield the deeply impactful insight being pointed toward in this exploration of possibilities. 

Here, insight feels like “knowing more.” It is structured, incremental, and attached to the self-object:
	•	It orbits around agency and the familiar logic of “me navigating the world.”
	•	It carries the texture of progress, always adding, refining, or improving conceptual maps.
	•	Its aim is often implicit: reduce discomfort, increase satisfaction.

This symbolic metacognition is not false — it’s the engine of science, discourse, and practical intelligence. But it is bound by its own fabric: words, concepts, constructs. Even when it points toward something deeper, it cannot itself step outside the circle it has drawn.


Everything Else - Thought Beyond Symbols:

With that brief outline of the nature of conceptual symbolic knowledge and how it alone often falls short of the profound possibilities we sense are possible, let’s turn to the deeper, far richer, and more subtle realm of subsymbolic metacognition, where pivotal moments of insight emerge as recognition of what is already here and was previously transparent to us. 

In contrast to deterministic knowing stands what appears to us as an enigmatic deep knowing that, when experienced, cannot be captured or relayed via language. Our most profound potential unfolds from penetrating insight. This domain of knowing is as spontaneous as it is transformative. 

Subsymbolic metacognition, the experience of “Knowing our knowing,” is an expansion of awareness, a spontaneous, insightful recognition of our own thought as thought and the origin of experience itself.

Direct experience is the playground of expanding awareness, and we encounter this potential for expansion often. When we do encounter this potential, we might see or call it being “stuck.” 

It’s here, something very different takes place. Insight is not about “knowing more” but about seeing freshly:
	•	It doesn’t accumulate but illuminates.
	•	It does not orbit the self-object, but reveals the transparent nature of the orbit itself.
	•	It is not incremental but transformative, discontinuous, spontaneous.

This subsymbolic metacognition is not about what is known, but the direct recognition of knowing itself. It is the mind turning luminous upon itself — a recognition that the very texture of experience (including the tacit sensations of thought) originates from within.

What was once seen as being stuck is, in truth, the threshold — the moment symbolic sense-making collapses and the space for a wholly different order of knowing emerges.


- Stuck is generally experienced as a perceived impasse + perceived catastrophic potential. 

This experience can be real or imagined, and often, we can’t distinguish our imagined representations from reality.

Recognizing this impasse within ourselves can be highly distressing, and just as easily, this distressing impasse can be a highly enjoyable playground of immense possibilities. Although the highly enjoyable playground is the less-traveled road, its potential significance cannot be stressed enough.

Embracing, savoring, and resting-into the direct experience of being ->stuck<- can be a profound and monumental shift. This shift to direct experience has the potential to become a penetrating space of insight, a metacognitive dojo, so to speak (a dojo is where one discovers the ‘way’ of nature unfolding). In this shift, we potentially discover our innate capacity. The innate and effortless space within us where we leap in understanding of what is already here. We see our own thinking that a moment ago looked so unquestionably real to us as absent of any reality, and certainly nothing we can get behind now.

That impasse experience can be a profound space of insight.

Exploring Directly
To explore being stuck, one does not analyze or fix. One simply turns toward the experience and notices:
•	What are the sensations that make this feel so real?
•	What story is thought layering over those sensations?
•	Who would I be if these sensations were not mistaken as “proof” of the story?
•	What if “stuck” is not an obstacle but an entryway — a dojo where the way of mind reveals itself?

When a rooster crows, a cat purrs, or a horse exhales into a whinny, nothing stands between impulse and expression. There’s no “translator” inside debating the right phrasing — the sound is the feeling. The creature and its voice arise as one event.

Humans, however, developed the extraordinary trick of turning sound into symbols — language — which allows us to describe, plan, imagine. But with that gift came a quiet split: sound no longer flows straight from being; it first passes through thought. Meaning becomes mediated. We can say how we feel without quite being the feeling. We have learned to stand apart from being. We fear being. Not because it’s dangerous, but because of what we believe about being. 

Every now and then, though, something breaks through — laughter, a sigh, the sound that slips out before words assemble. In that instant, communication becomes as direct as the rooster’s crow: no concept, just resonance from our being. That resonance is the shared field beneath all talk, the way awareness itself hums in both speaker and listener.

When we truly listen — not to the words, but to the life vibrating through them — language dissolves back into what it always was: living sound, meaning already understood before it’s named. Without our thinking mitigation, insight spontaneously emerges, our being is moved.
