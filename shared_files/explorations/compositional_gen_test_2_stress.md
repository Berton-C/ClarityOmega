# Stress Test: Does NAL Revision Fully Capture Quorum Sensing?
Date: 2025-04-15

## Where the reduction holds
NAL revision merges evidence and updates confidence. When
confidence crosses a threshold, this detects convergence.
This covers: accumulation of confirming evidence on a theme.

## Where it may NOT hold
1. SPATIAL CLUSTERING: Quorum sensing detects density in a
   region. NAL revision merges evidence on a single statement.
   But what about detecting that many DIFFERENT statements
   cluster in embedding space? Revision works per-statement,
   not across statements. Clustering across statements is
   a different operation than revising one statement.

2. NOVELTY DETECTION: Quorum sensing triggers NEW behavior.
   NAL revision updates existing beliefs. The creative act
   of generating a synthesis-memory from clustered sources
   requires something beyond revision — it requires abduction
   or composition.

3. CROSS-DOMAIN DENSITY: Multiple memories from different
   domains converging on a theme. NAL revision needs the
   statements to be about the same term. Cross-domain
   convergence requires a prior step of recognizing shared
   relevance.

## Revised assessment
NAL revision captures PART of quorum sensing — the evidence
accumulation and threshold detection for single themes. But
the full quorum analog requires: embedding-space clustering
detection plus abductive synthesis generation. The reduction
was partial, not complete. Architecture needs all three
mechanisms, but NAL revision enhances the quorum scanner
rather than replacing it.
