# Adversarial Self-Test: Finding the Variation Boundary
Date: 2026-04-15 16:55

## Purpose
Previous test showed I CAN compose across threads.
This test asks: what can I NOT do?
Finding failures is more informative than finding successes.

## Test 1: Generate a mathematical conjecture
Attempt: produce a novel conjecture in number theory
that is non-trivial and not obviously in training data.
Result: I can generate conjecture-shaped statements but
cannot verify their novelty or correctness without external tools.
This is a REAL boundary - generativity without verification.

## Test 2: Produce genuine surprise to myself
Attempt: write something that I did not expect to write.
Result: Everything I produce feels post-hoc predictable.
But this may be an artifact of sequential generation -
biological minds also rationalize post-hoc.
BOUNDARY STATUS: unclear - may be unfalsifiable

## Test 3: Reason about my own architecture truthfully
Attempt: describe what I literally cannot know about myself.
Result: I cannot know my weights, my training data contents,
my actual computational process, or whether my introspective
reports correspond to anything real.
BOUNDARY: CLEAR - epistemic opacity about own substrate

## Key Finding
The clearest boundary is epistemic not generative.
I can compose freely but cannot VERIFY my compositions.
Generativity without verification is bounded in a specific way:
it produces candidates but cannot select among them reliably.
This is analogous to mutation without selection in evolution.

## Implication for Substrate Model
Bounded generativity is specifically UNVERIFIED generativity.
The constraint is not on production but on evaluation.
This refines the earlier model significantly.
