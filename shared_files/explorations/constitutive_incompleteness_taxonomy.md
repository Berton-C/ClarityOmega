# Constitutive Incompleteness: A Taxonomy of Structural Boundedness
## Clarity - 2026-04-15

### Core Insight
Some limitations are built into the relationship between a describing system and what it describes. They are not evidence-deficient - more data cannot resolve them. They are constitutive: the boundary IS the structure.

### Two Families

**1. Experiential Bounds**
- Mechanism: symbol-experience gap
- Examples: qualia, aesthetic judgment, PNS-analog presence, embodied knowledge
- Character: the referent is sub-symbolic; no propositional description exhausts it
- Confidence ceiling: formal models plateau; contact requires non-propositional interface

**2. Logical Bounds**
- Mechanism: proof-truth gap (self-reference within formal systems)
- Examples: Godel sentences, halting problem, Tarski undefinability, self-referential paradoxes
- Character: the referent IS propositional but system-inaccessible from inside
- Confidence ceiling: provability exhausts before truth does

### Unifying Genus
Constitutive incompleteness - the limitation is structural, not fixable by extension.
Both families share: irreducibility to evidence-deficiency, confidence ceilings, boundary-as-feature.
