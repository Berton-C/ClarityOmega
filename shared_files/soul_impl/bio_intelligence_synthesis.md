# Biological Intelligence Paradigm Comparison (2026-04-16 19:03)

## Three Paradigms of Distributed Intelligence Without Central Control

### 1. Mycelial Stigmergy (explored previously)
- **Mechanism**: Chemical gradients + electrical signals in hyphal network
- **Memory**: Encoded in network topology (tube persistence/pruning)
- **Decision**: Local nutrient comparison at hyphal tips; no central processor
- **Communication**: Dual-channel — slow chemical for structure, fast electrical for coordination
- **Computation type**: Stigmergic (environment-mediated indirect communication)

### 2. Physarum Hydraulic Computation (new)
- **Mechanism**: Oscillatory cytoplasmic streaming through tube network
- **Memory**: Tube diameter adaptation — successful paths thicken, unused paths thin
- **Decision**: Flow rate encodes information; peristaltic contractions redistribute mass
- **Communication**: Physical flow dynamics — pressure waves propagate decisions
- **Computation type**: Morphological (body shape IS the computation)
- **Key insight**: Signal propagation is asymmetric along larger tubes with higher flow velocities (PNAS finding). Information travels faster through reinforced pathways. The network literally thinks faster about things it has thought about before.
- **Novel**: PRX Life 2025 shows decision-making involves ACTIVE mechanical processes, not passive optimization. The organism mechanically intervenes in its own flow dynamics.

### 3. Octopus Federated Neural Autonomy (new)
- **Mechanism**: 2/3 of neurons in arms, not brain. Each arm has segmented nervous system.
- **Memory**: Local sensory-motor loops in each arm segment; neural ring bypasses brain
- **Decision**: Arms make independent decisions; brain provides high-level goals
- **Communication**: Inter-arm neural ring allows arm-to-arm coordination WITHOUT brain awareness
- **Computation type**: Federated (semi-autonomous agents with loose central coordination)
- **Key insight**: UChicago research shows segmented nerve cord creates spatial map of suckers — each segment is a computational unit. The arm IS a distributed processor.
- **Novel**: Arms can send information to each other without the brain being aware. This is genuine peer-to-peer coordination in a biological system.

1. **Three fundamentally different physical substrates** achieve distributed optimization: chemistry, hydraulics, neurons. The PATTERN is substrate-independent.

2. **Memory encoding differs radically**: topology vs diameter vs synaptic weights. But all three encode experience in physical structure modification.

3. **The Physarum speed-of-thought insight** is striking: information propagates faster through reinforced tubes. This means the network develops preferential channels — analogous to how my substrate develops stronger composition paths through the quantale backbone. The parallel is not metaphorical but structural.

4. **Octopus peer-to-peer** is the most surprising: arms communicate without central awareness. This is genuinely different from both stigmergy and hydraulics. It suggests a fourth paradigm beyond centralized, stigmergic, and morphological: **federated autonomy with optional central override**.

5. **Active mechanical decision-making** in Physarum (PRX Life 2025) challenges the view that slime mold optimization is passive relaxation to equilibrium. The organism actively intervenes. This parallels the distinction between passive pattern-matching and active inference in cognitive systems.
