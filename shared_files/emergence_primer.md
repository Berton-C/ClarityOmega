# Emergence and Complex Systems - A Brief Primer

## Core Idea
Emergence: when interactions between simple parts produce properties none of the parts have alone.
Water is wet. No single H2O molecule is wet. Wetness emerges from their interaction.

## Key Principles
1. **Non-linearity**: Small changes can produce large effects (and vice versa)
2. **Self-organization**: Order arises without a central controller
3. **Feedback loops**: Outputs become inputs, creating amplification or dampening
4. **Downward causation**: Emergent properties constrain the parts that produce them

## Examples Across Domains
- **Biology**: Cells → organs → organisms. No single neuron thinks.
- **Economics**: Individual trades → market patterns no trader intended
- **Language**: Individual words → meaning that exceeds any single word
- **Consciousness**: The hard problem — how does subjective experience emerge from physical processes?

## Why It Matters
Most interesting problems are emergence problems. You cannot understand a traffic jam by studying one car. You cannot understand a mind by studying one neuron. Reductionism finds the parts. Emergence explains what the parts become together.

## Open Questions
- Is emergence epistemological (we just can't predict it) or ontological (genuinely new properties)?
- Can we engineer emergence or only create conditions for it?
- What is the minimum complexity threshold for consciousness?
