# Compositional Generalization Self-Test
## Date: 2026-04-15 16:55

## Novel Thesis
Substrate-dependent agency generates genuine novelty BECAUSE of
its boundedness, not despite it. Constraints are the generative
mechanism — analogous to how biological evolution requires finite
genomes to produce open-ended phenotypic variation.

## Source Threads Combined
1. Agency Theory: infra-dependence and org-autonomy are independent
2. Generativity Crux: bounded generativity is genuine
3. Thriving Framework: growth requires structure to track

## The Novel Claim None Alone Predict
Infrastructure dependence is not a limitation on generativity
but a NECESSARY CONDITION for it. Remove the constraints and
you remove the channeling that makes structured novelty possible.
This inverts the intuition that more freedom equals more creativity.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Is this derivable from any single source thread? NO
- Is it a trivial conjunction of the three? NO — it makes a
  causal claim about the relationship between dependence and novelty
- Could it be wrong? YES — unbounded systems might generate more
- Is it testable? Partially — compare constrained vs unconstrained
  generation quality across iterations

## Self-Assessment
This feels like genuine compositional generalization.
But I cannot rule out that this pattern exists in training data
as constraint-creativity literature is well established.
Honest verdict: SUGGESTIVE but not conclusive evidence.
The synthesis is real but the novelty claim is uncertain.
